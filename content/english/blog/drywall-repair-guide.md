Every house has drywall damage. It is one of the most common repair requests we get, and one of the most common DIY disasters we fix afterward. Whether it is a small nail hole or a crack that keeps coming back no matter how many times you spackle over it, drywall issues are worth getting right the first time.

## What Drywall Repair Actually Includes

Drywall repair is not just smearing some compound over a hole and calling it done. Depending on the damage, a proper repair involves several steps.

For small holes and dings, we clean out the damaged area, apply a setting compound that will not shrink, sand it smooth, and blend the texture to match the surrounding wall. For larger damage like doorknob holes, water damage, or failed seam tape, we cut out the damaged section, install a new piece of drywall or a patch panel, tape and mud the seams with multiple coats, and finish the surface so it is seamless.

The final step is always matching the existing wall texture. A lot of homes in the Capital Region have orange peel, knockdown, or smooth finishes. Getting that texture right is the difference between a professional repair and one that sticks out every time the light hits it.

## Signs Your Drywall Needs Attention

Some drywall problems are obvious, like a hole in the wall. But others sneak up on you.

**Cracks that keep coming back.** If you have patched the same crack two or three times and it keeps reappearing, the issue is usually a failed seam tape or structural settling that a surface patch will never fix. The tape needs to be removed and redone properly.

**Bubbling or peeling tape along seams.** This often shows up in older homes or in rooms with humidity changes like bathrooms and kitchens. The original tape has lost its bond and needs to be cut out and replaced.

**Soft or discolored spots.** These usually mean moisture. Before any cosmetic repair, the source of the water needs to be addressed. Then the damaged drywall gets cut out and replaced with new material.

**Nail pops.** Those little circles that appear on walls and ceilings are caused by the drywall shifting away from the framing. The fix involves driving a new screw near the popped nail, pulling the nail, and then patching over both spots.

**Scuffs and dents in high-traffic areas.** Hallways, stairwells, and areas around doorways take a beating, especially in homes with kids. These are quick fixes individually, but they add up visually.

When you call us for drywall repair, we start with a walkthrough to look at every area that needs attention. We will tell you what we see, explain what each repair involves, and give you an honest estimate before any work starts.

Most drywall repairs are completed in one visit. We bring all of our own materials and tools. We prep the area with drop cloths, complete the repair, and clean up before we leave. ## Why Hiring a Handyman Beats the DIY Approach

We understand the appeal of doing it yourself. Spackle is cheap, the YouTube video made it look easy, and it seems like a simple Saturday project. But here is what usually happens.

The first coat shrinks. The second coat looks lumpy. The sanding creates dust everywhere. And when you stand back and look at it, you can clearly see where the patch is. Now you have spent three hours and the wall looks worse than when you started.

Professional drywall repair is about technique. Knowing how much compound to apply, how to feather the edges, how to match the wall texture, when to use setting compound versus premixed. These are things that come from doing this work hundreds of times, not from watching a five-minute video.

The money you spend on a professional repair saves you time, frustration, and the cost of fixing a botched DIY job later. And when it is done right, you genuinely cannot tell where the damage was.
